Completed

These deals have gone through all the necessary steps and have been completed.

Brightly Acquired By Siemens
Industrial-manufacturing conglomerate Siemens AG finalized its acquisition of Brightly Software, Inc. from Clearlake Capital Group LP for $1.575 billion. Brightly provides cloud-based enterprise asset management and facility operations management for companies through a software-as-a-service model. The company has grown its business considerably since Clearlake took ownership in 2019 and currently has more than 12,000 customers across education, public infrastructure, manufacturing, and healthcare. Brightly will join Siemens’ Intelligent Infrastructure division and help to advance Siemens’ position as a leading provider of software for buildings and infrastructure.
Right Stuf Acquired By Crunchyroll
A year after it was acquired by Sony Corporation’s (NYSE: SONY) FUNimation Entertainment Ltd, anime streaming giant Crunchyroll announced that it has acquired anime merchandise retailer Right Stuf, Inc for an undisclosed sum. The move represents a large consolidating move by Sony, though Crunchyroll said it will continue to stock releases from other North American distributors. Right Stuf is the largest retailer of anime, manga, and related merchandise in North America. Earlier this year Sony merged FUNimation’s and Crunchyroll’s streaming catalogs into one service; the acquisition of Right Stuf signals a move to become a one-stop shop for anime fans to buy and stream from. Anime has exploded in popularity internationally over the past five years, going from a minor subculture to a major cultural phenomenon with an estimated market of almost $25 billion.
Metromile Acquired By Lemonade
Lemonade (NYSE: LMND), the artificial intelligence-powered insurance company, announced that it has closed the acquisition of car insurance provider Metromile, Inc. (NASDAQ: MILE). San Francisco-based Metromile offers pay-per-mile car insurance and licenses its digital insurance platform to other insurance companies. The company went public via a special purpose acquisition company in February 2021 at a valuation of $1 billion, but its valuation dropped to $137 million after the deal closed. For Lemonade, which launched its own insurance product just a few months ago, the acquisition of Metromile’s sensor and monitor technologies along with billions of miles of driving data and hundreds of thousands of claims data will make its Lemonade Car platform “the most competitive, precise, and fair.”

Pending

These deals have been announced, but are still subject to varying levels of approval. While it is likely they will eventually close, there is a chance they may be called off or altered. There are also updates to previously announced deals if notable.

Apollo To Acquire Atlas Air
Airfreight service Atlas Air Worldwide (NASDAQ: AAWW) announced that it agreed to be acquired by Apollo Global Management, Inc. (NYSE: APO) for $3.2 billion. Atlas specializes in transporting perishables, heavy industrial equipment, and other goods and is a global leader in airfreight whose customers include Boeing Co, FedEx Corp, and MotoGP, among others. Apollo sees the acquisition as an opportunity to capitalize on the market demand for air-cargo services as the needs of the global supply chain grow. The deal is expected to close in Q4 2022 or Q1 2023, pending regulatory approval. After closing AAWW will be delisted and become a privately held company.
TD Bank To Buy Cowen
Canadian banking firm Toronto Dominion Bank (TD) announced that it plans to acquire New York-based investment bank Cowen Inc. (NASDAQ: COWN) for $1.3 billion. TD has been looking to expand its market reach beyond Canada in recent years and the acquisition is expected to help diversify the firm’s capital-markets business in the United States, though analysts warn that the integration of Cowen could cause headaches due to management and cultural differences between the two companies. Funds for Cowen will be procured through the sale of shares from TD’s Charles Schwab investment services firm. The Cowen deal is TD’s second this year and is expected to close in Q1 2023.

Estée Lauder In Talks To Buy Tom Ford
According to the Wall Street Journal, cosmetics giant Estée Lauder Cos (NYSE: EL) is in talks to acquire luxury fashion brand Tom Ford in a deal potentially worth $3 billion. Bloomberg first reported in early July that Tom Ford was for sale. Per people familiar with the matter, there are no indications that an agreement could be reached at this juncture, as Estée Lauder isn’t the only suitor. Though known for its menswear, Tom Ford also sells womenswear and accessories, as well as a fast-growing, high-end line of cosmetics and fragrances. Estée Lauder is particularly interested in its beauty business and the two companies already have a long standing licensing deal. If successful the deal will become Estée Lauder’s biggest acquisition to date, following the company’s announced $2.2 billion acquisition of Deciem in 2021.
Signify Health Exploring Alternatives
According to the Wall Street Journal, Signify Health Inc. (NYSE: SGFY) is working with investment bankers to explore strategic alternatives including a sale, less than 2 years after its initial public offering. Founded in 2017, Signify Health is a healthcare-services company that supplies technology solutions for in-home care health plans and providers. After raising $500 million from its IPO in early 2021, the company’s stock has fallen 25% since then, and it has announced in July that it plans to close one of its units after recent changes to a government payment model. Per people familiar with the matter, a deal is still far from guaranteed, and the company could attract offers from different managed care plans and private equity groups. New York-based private-equity firm New Mountain Capital has been an investor since 2017.

Penguin-Simon & Schuster Merger Trial Begins
The proposed merger between publishing houses Penguin Random House LLC and Simon & Schuster, Inc. is in court this week as the Department of Justice is suing to stop what it argues is a monopolizing move in an industry already dominated by a few major players. Penguin and Simon & Schuster argue that the merger will help “enhance” competition among publishers and force them to compete harder for hot titles. The trial is expected to last through the middle of this month, with a final verdict announced by November. Simon & Schuster’s parent company, Paramount Global (NASDAQ: PARA) has been looking to offload the publisher and may wind up selling it to private investors if the merger is blocked.
Pepsi Invests In Celsius
PepsiCo, Inc. (NASDAQ: PEP) announced that it has made a $550 million, or roughly 8.5% stake, investment in energy drink maker Celsius Holdings Inc. (NASDAQ: CELH) as part of a long-term distribution deal. Founded in 2004, Celsius offers sugar-free and caffeinated sparkling beverages, and markets itself as a healthy alternative to diet sodas and other energy drinks. The company experienced explosive growth and gained name recognition during the pandemic with its successful social media marketing on TikTok and Instagram. Energy drinks are one of the fastest growing categories in the non-alcoholic beverage segment, and this deal would strengthen Pepsi’s presence in this category. In early 2020, Pepsi bought legacy energy drink maker Rockstar for $3.85 billion.
